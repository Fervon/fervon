What happened

Why Rewind AI no longer exists

Rewind AI was one of the genuinely clever products of the past few years: a Mac app that recorded everything you did on your computer and let you search it. Forgot where you read that statistic? What was said in that call? You searched and found it. It was a genuinely good idea.

The company then pivoted to a wearable (the Limitless pendant), rebranded as Limitless, and was acquired by Meta in 2025. The Rewind desktop app was shut down on December 19, 2025. For all the users who relied on it for a local, searchable record of their workday, the tool simply stopped existing.

This page exists to talk about what to do now — especially if you wanted something like Rewind but on Windows or Linux, where Rewind never ran to begin with.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is the best Rewind AI alternative in 2026?

The main options are Screenpipe (open-source, heavy screen recording), OpenRecall (open-source, screenshot-based), and Trace by Fervon (local-first, no screen recording, cross-platform). Trace is the closest match to what Rewind offered — a searchable personal memory — without continuous screen recording or cloud dependency.

What happened to Rewind AI?

Rewind AI was acquired by Meta and its desktop app was shut down on December 19, 2025. The company had previously rebranded as Limitless and pivoted to a wearable pendant device. The original local desktop memory tool users relied on stopped working after the shutdown.

Is there a Rewind alternative that works on Windows and Linux?

Yes. Rewind AI was Mac-only. Trace by Fervon runs on Windows, macOS and Linux. It captures browser history, active window and clipboard without screen recording — making it accessible to the majority of users who were never able to run Rewind at all.

Does Trace require screen recording like Screenpipe?

No. Trace captures lightweight signals — browser history, active window title, clipboard text — instead of recording screen video. This makes Trace much lighter on CPU and RAM, safe for work machines, and free of the privacy concerns that come with storing continuous screen footage.

Is there a one-time payment Rewind alternative?

Yes. Trace is a one-time purchase — $39 early-bird lifetime (regular $59). Rewind charged a recurring subscription, and most cloud-flavoured memory tools still do. Trace gives you the desktop app forever for a single payment, with no recurring fees.
