FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is Rewind still available?

No. After Meta acquired Limitless, the Rewind desktop app was shut down on December 19, 2025. If you relied on it for a searchable memory of your day, you now need a replacement — which is exactly the gap Trace fills.

What's the best replacement for Rewind now?

It depends on what you valued about Rewind. If you want a quiet, finished app that gives you a searchable memory of your day without recording video, Trace is the closest independent, local-first option. Screenpipe and OpenRecall still record the screen continuously, and Microsoft Recall only runs on Copilot+ PCs. Trace captures light signals instead — browser history, the active window and clipboard text.

Does Trace record my screen like Rewind?

No. Unlike Rewind, Trace does not record your screen. It captures small signals — browser history, the active window and clipboard text — instead of recording video around the clock, so it sits quietly in the background and stays lightweight on an ordinary computer.

Is Trace free?

No, but there's no subscription. Trace is a one-time purchase. Joining the waitlist locks in the $39 early-bird lifetime price (regular $59), with future updates included. Unlike Rewind, there's no cloud to fund, so there's nothing to bill you for monthly.

Which platforms does Trace support?

Trace is built local-first for Windows, macOS and Linux. It runs entirely on your machine — no servers, no accounts, no telemetry — and you can confirm nothing leaves your computer by running it with your firewall closed.
